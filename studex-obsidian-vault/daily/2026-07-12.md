## 📊 RESEARCH FINDINGS (Research Hour — 10:00 PM SAST)

### Topic 1: China Coffee Market — July 20 Open Window is REAL, But Uganda Moved First

**The Policy:**
- **July 20, 2026:** China opens to coffee beans from all 53 African countries under a unified phytosanitary framework.
- Zero tariffs on African coffee already active (eliminated mid-2025). FOB price is the only barrier.
- Single approval replaces 53 separate country-by-country protocols — administrative friction just collapsed.

**Who's Buying in China:**
- **Luckin Coffee** — 20,000+ stores, world's largest coffee chain by store count. Rwanda washed A1 fits their house blend at ~USD 4.80/kg FOB.
- **Tim Hortons China** — 500+ stores, Canadian chain. Larger particle = A2 natural grade viable.
- **JD.com / FreshHema** — E-commerce import channels. Most realistic first contacts for a small exporter without a China office.
- **Heytea** — Premium tea chain. Skip.
- ⚠️ Uganda is already actively courting Chinese buyers — Coffee Bash, Alibaba-matched buyers, MICE platform. Rwanda needs to move fast.

**China Buyer Reality:**
- Chinese roasters prefer **container-scale orders** (1,000kg+). Price must compete on cup quality.
- Trust is the core barrier. PROWTC / Rainforest Alliance certs = credibility signal.
- CIIE November 2026 (Shanghai) — still the single best opportunity. Booth cost ~USD 3,000-8,000.

**UAE / GCC Headwinds:**
- Red Sea shipping reroutes add 2-3 weeks, freight costs up significantly, tourism demand softer.
- Hotel groups remain reliable buyers — reliability over specialty.
- Freight disruption favors proximity suppliers. South Africa via Durban is a safer route for SA-roasted Rwandan green bean.

### Topic 2: SA B2B Coffee — Hotels, Restaurants, Offices

**Market Size:**
- SA out-of-home coffee market: ~$1.94B (2026), growing at 3.14% CAGR.
- B2B wholesale = 63% of global coffee market.

**Who the Buyers Are + How They Source:**
- **Hotels (4-5 star):** Procurement via GPOs (Avendra) or direct from large distributors (Tribe, Fiusis). 1-2 year contracts. Price-insensitive on quality, highly sensitive on reliability.
- **Restaurants / Casual dining:** Via foodservice distributors (Bidfood, Foodsite, Caterhub). Price-sensitive. Switching costs low.
- **Offices (corporate coffee):** Dominated by service providers who own the equipment. White space: offices that want to own premium bean + equipment.
- **Hospitals, universities, mining camps:** Large tenders, commoditized. Not Studex's target.

**Top SA Coffee Buyer Pain Points:**
1. **Reliability of supply** — load shedding disrupts SA roasting. Buyers want supplier who won't miss Monday morning delivery.
2. **Traceability expectations rising** — hotel groups increasingly asked "where is this coffee from?" Farm-level provenance is a sales tool.
3. **Equipment + training捆绑** — buyers want a partner who manages the whole coffee experience.
4. **Price volatility** — arabica futures elevated ($2.50-3.00/lb). Buyers want fixed-price contracts.
5. **Load shedding risk** — SA roasters on generators pass costs to buyers. Rwandan green bean roasted in SA avoids this = cost advantage.
