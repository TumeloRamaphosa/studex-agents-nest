## 🇿🇦 South Africa Coffee Market — B2B Targets

### Who Buys in Bulk?
1. **Hospitality groups** — Tsogo Sun, Southern Sun, Sun International (hotels/casinos)
   - Each hotel group: 50–200kg/week ground coffee
   - Procurement cycles: annual tender
2. **Restaurant chains** — Spur, RocoMamas, Fishaways, Panarottis
   - Standard supply agreements, 30-day payment terms
3. **Office coffee service** — Coffee Junction, Vida e Caffè, Disruptors (Truckstop, Raza)
   - Monthly subscription, recurring revenue model
4. **Retail** — Pick n Pay, Woolworths, Checkers
   - Private label opportunity — biggest volume
5. **Wholesale distributors** — Bidfood, Foodsite, Caterwell
   - B2B2C model — they distribute to smaller outlets

### SA Coffee Import Regulations
- **Department of Agriculture:** Import permit required for green (raw) coffee
- **SARS (customs):** 15% import duty + VAT on green coffee
- **COA required:** Certificate of Origin (for preferential tariff under AfCFTA)
- **Fumigation:** Required per ISPM standards — supplier handles FOB, buyer handles on arrival

### Subscription Business Model
- **Roasted beans** — grind to order, ship within 24 hours
- **Wholesale bags (1kg, 2kg)** — delivered to offices/restaurants monthly
- **D2C subscription boxes** — 250g, 500g, 1kg monthly delivery via The一夜间
- **Margin benchmark:** Buy green at USD 4,500/MT → roast → sell at R280–450/kg → **3.5–4.5x markup**
