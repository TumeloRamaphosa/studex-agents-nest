South Africa's foodservice industry generated **USD 5.5 billion (≈R102 billion)** in revenue in 2023, a 27% increase year-on-year, with the high-end restaurant segment outperforming the broader market as affluent consumers continued dining out despite inflationary pressure. This document is a full, actionable go-to-market plan targeting that segment.

The goal: **5 paying wholesale restaurant accounts within 90 days**, building to **R150,000 MRR from wholesale alone**.

## 2. Target Tiers

### Tier 1 — Established Steakhouses (50–200 kg/month premium beef)

These are the highest-volume anchor clients. They operate structured procurement, have established meat suppliers, and require competitive pricing, consistent grading, and cold-chain reliability. The opportunity is to **displace or augment a line item** — introduce Wagyu as a premium upsell cut rather than replacing the whole supply relationship on Day 1.

**Profile:** Independent or small-chain steakhouse with 80+ covers, dedicated grill section, existing premium beef menu. Monthly meat spend R60,000–R200,000. Decision-maker: owner-operator or F&B manager.

**Key entry point:** Wagyu ribeye or Wagyu sirloin as a featured special, priced 2–3× their current premium cuts. A single weekly special using 10–15 kg Wagyu can generate R6,000–R12,000 in menu revenue — easily justifying a trial.

### Tier 2 — Upscale Burger & Gastropub (30–80 kg/month)

Fast-growing segment. Wagyu burger patties are the natural entry point — premium positioning at accessible price (R220–R350 per burger on menu). Monthly meat spend R20,000–R60,000 on beef alone. Decision-maker typically owner-chef.

**Key entry point:** 2 kg sample of Wagyu burger patties + cost-per-patty calculation showing menu profitability at R300 per 180g Wagyu burger.

### Tier 3 — Hotel F&B + Private Clubs (variable, 20–100 kg/month)

Procurement cycle is longer (6–12 weeks to get on an approved supplier list), but the contracts are sticky and often multi-year. Hotels with 4-star+ ratings and an a la carte restaurant are the target. The Halaal certification is a decisive advantage here — most major hotel chains (Marriott, Sun International, Protea/Marriott) have significant Muslim guest volumes and require Halaal-certified proteins.

**Key entry point:** Get on the approved supplier list via the F&B manager or Executive Chef. Offer to do a staff tasting.

### Tier 4 — Catering & Dark Kitchens (variable, 10–60 kg/month)

Lower margin, higher volume. Include premium event caterers (corporate, wedding), dark kitchen operators on Uber Eats / Mr D who market premium meal kits. Good for moving volume cuts (Wagyu rump, biltong bulk packs) at Tier prices.

#### Three-Tier Wholesale Programme

| Tier | Name | Monthly Volume Threshold | Discount vs. List | Credit Terms | Delivery | MOQ |
|------|------|--------------------------|-------------------|-------------|----------|-----|
| **Silver** | Entry | R8,000–R20,000/month | 10% off list | 7-day EFT | Weekly | R2,500 per order |
| **Gold** | Growth | R20,001–R60,000/month | 18% off list | 14-day EFT | Twice-weekly | R5,000 per order |
| **Platinum** | Partner | R60,001+/month | 25% off list + co-branding | 30-day account | On-demand (48hr notice) | R10,000 per order |

**Notes:**
- All pricing is ex-VAT; 15% VAT applies to processed/cooked products (biltong); raw meat cuts are zero-rated for VAT.
- Credit terms escalate with account age: new accounts start on 7-day terms; after 3 months consistent payment, upgrade to Gold/Platinum terms.
- Cold-chain delivery required: StudEx must use refrigerated transport (external courier or own vehicle). Standard refrigerated courier rates in SA are R150–R400 per drop (urban metro).
- Price review: quarterly, pegged to RMIS A2 carcass index.

### Where StudEx Differentiates

1. **Halaal-certified Wagyu** — The combination of Halaal certification and genuine Wagyu marbling is unique in SA. Express Meats has Wagyu but is not a Halaal specialist in the premium context. Excellent Meat is Halaal but not Wagyu. StudEx owns this intersection.

2. **Biltong as a restaurant ingredient** — Wagyu biltong boards and amuse-bouche are a growing fine-dining trend. No volume wholesaler offers premium Wagyu biltong as a restaurant ingredient line item. This is an untapped category.

3. **Founder-led brand story** — Premium buyers in SA (especially chef-owner independents) are story-driven. They want to know where the animal comes from and who stands behind the product. Large volume wholesalers (Express Meats, Karan Beef) have no founder narrative.

4. **Flexibility on custom cuts and packaging** — Volume wholesalers are inflexible. A steakhouse wanting 250g Wagyu ribeye portions, individually vacuum-sealed with the farm name on the pack, will not get that from Express Meats. StudEx can.

## 9. CRM + Operations Setup

### Recommendation: Notion-Based CRM (Start) → Supabase (Scale)

**Phase 1 (0–90 days): Notion CRM**

Given the existing Notion connector available in the tech stack, build a lightweight pipeline database in Notion. This requires zero cost, is fast to set up, and handles the 30–50 prospect volume easily.

**Pipeline Stages:**
1. `Lead` — Identified, not yet contacted
2. `Contacted` — Email/DM/call sent, awaiting response
3. `Qualified` — Confirmed interest, has decision-making authority
4. `Sample Sent` — Physical sample box dispatched or delivered
5. `Tasting Booked` — In-person tasting/meeting scheduled
6. `First Order` — Paid their first invoice
7. `Recurring` — 2nd+ order; now an ongoing account
8. `Churned` — Stopped ordering

**Notion Database Schema:**

| Field | Type | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| Restaurant Name | Title | |
| City | Select | Cape Town / Joburg / Durban / Pretoria |
| Tier | Select | 1 / 2 / 3 / 4 |
| Contact Name | Text | Decision-maker name |
| Contact Role | Select | Chef / F&B Manager / Owner |
| Email | Email | |
| Phone | Phone | |
| LinkedIn | URL | |
| Stage | Select | Lead → Recurring |
| Last Activity Date | Date | |
| Next Action | Text | What to do next |
| Monthly Volume (kg) | Number | Once ordering |
| Monthly Revenue (R) | Number | Once ordering |
| Notes | Text | Free text |
| Sample Sent | Checkbox | |
| Sample Sent Date | Date | |

**Phase 2 (90 days+): Supabase**

Once 10+ accounts are active and the data model is stable, migrate to a Supabase Postgres backend with a simple custom dashboard. Supabase is already in the connected tech stack. Benefits at scale:
- SQL queries for revenue forecasting by tier/city
- Automated delivery scheduling integration
- Invoice tracking and payment status
- API integration with delivery providers

**Alternative: HubSpot Free CRM** — If the team has no technical capacity, HubSpot Free is a solid fallback. It handles up to 1 million contacts, has built-in deal pipeline tracking, email templates, and call logging. No coding required.

**Recommendation:** Start with Notion for speed; migrate to Supabase after 90 days when pipeline is proven.
