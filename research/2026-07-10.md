## 📍 TOPIC 1: COFFEE MARKET — CHINA + UAE

### Key Findings

**China Opens July 20 — This Is CONFIRMED**
- China opens market to coffee beans from all 53 African countries with diplomatic ties, effective **July 20, 2026**
- **Single unified phytosanitary framework** — no bilateral negotiations required per country. This is new. It means Rwanda coffee can enter China the same day as Kenya, Ethiopia, or Zambia with the same paperwork
- **Zero-tariff** treatment already active since May 1, 2026 for all 53 countries
- **Green channel mechanism** — fast-track customs for safe, high-quality African agricultural products — coffee included
- Rwanda's A1/A2 green coffee is eligible from Day 1

**China Demand Is Surging**
- China's coffee consumption grew **21% annually since 2010** — a compounding structural trend, not a fad
- Key buyers: Luckin Coffee (17,000+ stores), Tim Hortons China, Pacific Coffee, JD.com, Yaoji Food, FreshHema (Alibaba)
- Luckin is the most reachable — they've been publicly sourcing African coffee and actively expanding premium lines
- No current SA-origin competitors in China that we know of — first-mover window is REAL

**UAE Market Intelligence**
- MENA coffee market: **USD 17.14 billion in 2026**, growing at **7.75% CAGR** to USD 24.89B by 2031
- Dubai green coffee import prices: **USD 4.41–10.84/kg** (massive range — quality differentiates at the top end)
- Dubai buyers prioritize: **distinctive flavor experiences, brand presentation, supply chain transparency, sustainability credentials**
- Solar-roasted / direct-trade / African origin = strong premium positioning in UAE hospitality
- UAE is NOT just a re-export hub — domestic consumption of specialty coffee is growing

**B2B SA Coffee — How Hotels/Restaurants/Offices Buy**
- Corporate office coffee market in SA shifting from "institutional machine brown liquid" → curated premium supply
- B2B buyers at premium hotels/restaurants want: quality story, sustainability credentials, staff training, reliability, tasting sessions
- Procurement is multi-stakeholder (chef/FOH manager/procurement officer) — needs a narrative, not just a price sheet
- B2B decision cycle is long — cupping sessions + trial periods before contract sign
- Snobs Coffee (local competitor) is actively targeting SA premium B2B — direct competitor positioning
- **Pain point we can exploit:** most SA B2B suppliers are transactional, not narrative-driven. Studex coffee has a better story (African luxury, Rwanda origin, wildlife-trade credentials)
