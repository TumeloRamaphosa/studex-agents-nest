## TOPIC 1: South Africa B2B Coffee — Hotels, Restaurants, Offices

### Key Findings

- **Market size**: Out-of-home coffee market worth USD 43.31B in 2026, growing at 4.11% CAGR. B2B channels dominate at ~60% of all roasted coffee procurement globally.
- **SA specialty coffee booming**: More cafés opening in major SA cities; events driving interest. SA imports most of its coffee — vulnerable to forex and supply fluctuations (a buyer advantage for established importers).
- **B2B buyer profile**:
  - *Hotels*: 4-5 star properties want single-origin, ethically sourced. Procurement via F&B suppliers (Bidfood, Food Lovers, Taste). Pain point: inconsistent quality between batches.
  - *Restaurants/Cafés*: Want custom blends, barista training support, equipment leasing. Pain point: load shedding disrupting service + rising operational costs.
  - *Corporate offices*: Moving to premium bean programs ( Nespresso Professional, Delonghi office). Pain point: no SA-specific luxury office coffee brand.
- **Price benchmarks**: Regular hot coffee median ~$3.74 globally (May 2026). Specialty single-serve in SA offices: R25–R40/cup equivalent. Premium B2B contracts: R180–R350/kg for blended beans.
- **Competitor positioning**: Cothas Coffee (custom blends for hotels/cafés), Snobs Coffee (solar-roasted, B2B push Feb 2026), independent specialty roasters undercutting multinationals. Space is fragmented — no SA luxury B2B coffee brand with export-grade wildlife/conservation story.

1. **Bundle coffee WITH meat**: Corporate gifting (hampers) — biltong + premium coffee is a natural pairing. Hot desk offices and corporate event caterers are buyers.

3. **Hotel supply**: Target 4-star boutique hotels in JHB/CPT via Bidfood/Food Lover's procurement. Offer trial packs.
