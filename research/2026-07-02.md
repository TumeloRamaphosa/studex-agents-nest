## TOPIC 1: African Coffee — China Just Opened Its Market (Historic)

### The Headline
**China eliminated tariffs on ALL imports from 53 African countries — effective May 1, 2026.**
Coffee beans specifically: was 8%+ tariff → **0%**. The Shanghai Customs "green channel" is now active, prioritizing rapid clearance for African coffee.

China further opens to coffee beans from ALL 53 African countries from **July 20, 2026** under a new unified phytosanitary system.

### What This Means
- China's coffee market: **$42 billion** (2024). Consumption grew **+150% since 2010**, at **21% annually** — faster than anywhere else on earth.
- Imports: 59,100 MT (2015) → 213,300 MT (2024). **Nearly 4x in 9 years.**
- Shanghai alone = 38% of China's total coffee import volume.
- **African suppliers now have cost parity with Latin American competitors for the first time.**

### Who's Buying in UAE
- MEA coffee market: **$17.14 billion (2026)**, growing at **7.75% CAGR** → $24.89B by 2031.
- World of Coffee Dubai 2026: **76+ producers** — largest in event history. Dubai is the re-export hub for African coffee into the Gulf.
- HORECA sector (hotels, restaurants, cafés) now demands **traceable origin** — the farm name, altitude, Rainforest Alliance cert is the entry ticket.
- **PROWTC Dubai connection:** Svetlana at PROWTC is positioned exactly right. The zero-tariff China policy makes Dubai re-export economics even stronger.

### Key Buyers Looking for African Coffee Right Now
| Buyer Type | What They Want | Price Point |
|---|---|---|
| Chinese import distributors | Large volumes, consistent quality, 8%+ cheaper now | FOB $3-6/kg |
| Dubai roasters / HORECA | Traceability story, Rainforest Alliance | Premium + 15-20% |
| European specialty | Single-origin, Cupping scores 84+, direct trade | FOB $6-12/kg |
| Gulf hotel groups | Supply contracts Dec-March for Q1, halal cert | Fixed price 6-12 mo |

### Key Bullish Signal for Studex Coffee
The China zero-tariff policy **validates African origin coffee as a strategic commodity**, not just a niche. Prices for African green coffee will rise as Chinese demand absorbs supply that previously went elsewhere. **Lock in Rwandan supply now before Chinese buyers push prices up.**
