## TOPIC 1: South Africa B2B Coffee — How Hotels, Restaurants & Offices Actually Buy

### How They Source

**Hotel Groups (Sun International, Tsogo Sun, City Lodge, Marriott SA)**
- Corporate procurement arms run tenders every 1-3 years. They use **foodservice distributors** (Bidfood, Foodcorp, Stellar Distribution) as the primary supply path — not direct from farms.
- Individual boutique hotels (Sandton, Cape Town, Mother City) are more accessible — often buy direct or via a local butcher/coffee roaster.
- Procurement trigger: new menu, chef change, sustainability certification push, or cost review.
- **Lead time matters:** hotels plan 3-6 months ahead for contracts. The window to pitch for Q4 supply is NOW.

**Restaurants & Gastro Pubs**
- Chefs decide. Not procurement managers. Build relationships with chefs/owners directly.
- Source via: local coffee roasters (Cape Town Coffee Roasters, Deluxe Coffee, Rosetta Roastery) OR direct from importers.
- Pain point: specialty restaurants want **single-origin story** to put on their menu. A Rwanda A1 with farm name + tasting notes is a menu item story, not just an ingredient.
- Ordering: small restaurants order weekly. Monthly contracts with net-30 payment terms are standard.

**Offices (Corporate Coffee)**
- Dominated by Arivia.kom, The Coffee Collective, and PnP's coffee service.
- SME offices are more accessible — order via WhatsApp or email, monthly delivery.
- Key driver: convenience + consistent quality. Not premium storytelling.
- **Not Studex's priority for coffee.** Meat is the better office play (client gifts, corporate hampers).

### Who to Target First (Priority Order)
1. **Boutique Sandton hotels** — highest traffic, most price-insensitive, want provenance story
2. **Cape Town gastro restaurants** — coffee-forward culture, Willing to pay premium for single-origin
3. **Export-facing lodges** (Kruger, Winelands) — international tourists expect premium local coffee
4. **Sun International / Tsogo Sun corporate** — hard to reach but huge volume. Best via distributor relationships.

### The Café Menu Angle
Restaurants put premium coffee on their menu as a **margin item** (200-400% markup). They buy at R150-300/kg and sell at R45-80/cup. This means they're very motivated buyers if the quality story is strong.

## TOPIC 2: Luxury Meat E-Commerce — Competitive Benchmark (SA Market)

### Key Players

**Woolworths Food Online** 🟡
- Price anchor: R180-350/kg for premium beef cuts. Full halal range. Cold-chain delivery (Woolies Dash). Loyalty programme (WRewards).
- **Studex gap:** Woolworths lacks farm-level traceability. They certify by abattoir, not farm. Studex can win here by going hyper-local (name the farm).
- Weakness: expensive, slow to adapt to DTC trends.

**Food Lover's Market** 🟡
- Price: R120-250/kg. Strong social media (Instagram-heavy). No halal certification — this is Studex's clearest differentiator in the Muslim consumer segment.
- **Studex gap:** No farm traceability, no halal, no agent-powered service. Studex wins on all three.

**SA Biltong (sabiltong.co.za)** 🟢
- Direct competitor in biltong DTC. Grass-fed, family-run, R499+/kg for standard biltong. Delivers nationwide in 1-4 days.
- **Their angle:** Authenticity + grass-fed claims. They do NOT have halal cert prominently. Studex should beat them on halal + South African provenance + agent-personalization.

**Block Men Beef (Instagram-based)** 🟢
- Specialists in premium Wagyu, Angus, Grass-Fed. Linden store (Johannesburg). Online ordering available.
- **Their angle:** Physical presence + premium positioning. Studex can match or beat on online-only model + national delivery + halal.

**Subscription Box Market — MASSIVE Opportunity**
- SA subscription box market: USD 305M (2025) → USD 1.2B by 2034. **16.3% CAGR.**
- **No major premium meat subscription in SA.** Food Lover's doesn't do it. Woolworths has a generic box. This is an open lane.
- **Studex Monthly Meat Box:** R499/month → curated biltong + beef cuts + droëwors. Subscriber = guaranteed recurring revenue. Low CAC because it's a subscription renewal, not a new acquisition each time.
