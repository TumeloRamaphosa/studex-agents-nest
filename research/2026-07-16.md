## Topic 1: South Africa B2B Coffee — Hotels, Restaurants, Offices

### Key Findings

- **Market size:** SA coffee market growing steadily; instant coffee still dominates commercial spaces (hotels, offices) but specialty is the growth vector. Market projected to expand through 2030 (BlueWeave Consulting).
- **Buyer profile — Hotels & Restaurants:** Procurement decision is driven by **consistency of supply**, **price stability**, and **cup quality**. Preference for local suppliers who can deliver at scale. International hotel chains (Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt SA) tend to use global preferred suppliers — hard to crack without volume track record.
- **Buyer profile — Offices:** Tend toward instant or pod systems (Nespresso-style). Pure-play offices are tough for specialty — price-sensitive, convenience-first. JSE-listed offices and corporate HQs are better targets (prestige factor).
- **Pain points (from industry reports):**
  - Climate change disrupting SA domestic supply
  - Land expropriation uncertainty spooking new agricultural investment
  - Price inflation (coffee prices hit 10-year high globally, per YouTube/industry reports)
  - Inconsistent quality from local roasters
- **CIIE November 2026 (Shanghai) is a prime target** — China coffee market estimated at **$42B (2024)**, consumption up 150% since baseline. Rwanda/African origin is a **differentiator** (origin story, not just commodity). South African-origin green coffee protocol with China effective **July 20, 2026** — this is NOW.
