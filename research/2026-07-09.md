## TOPIC 1: Luxury Meat E-Commerce SA — Competitors & Pricing

### Key Findings

- **SA e-commerce market:** Growing at 8.17% CAGR → $77.27B by 2034. Food e-commerce globally at 20.3% CAGR. Studex is positioned at the right time.
- **Competitor landscape:**
  - **biltong.co.za** — established SA biltong online store with physical branches
  - **Noags.co.za** — Wagyu beef, R600/kg rump, R950/case. Lower price tier than Studex. Targets value-conscious buyers.
  - **Angus Meat Supply** — WhatsApp + online, "quality meat at not-expensive prices." Direct competitor on convenience.
  - **Takealot** — dominant SA marketplace. Listing fees $350–$3,500/year per product line. High barrier.
- **Studex pricing benchmark:** Premium Wagyu biltong 200g at R450. At R2,250/kg — roughly **3.7x Noags' beef price**. Justified by: Halaal certification, farm-to-door, Ankole heritage story, gifting positioning.
- **Critical gap:** Most SA online meat sellers compete on price or convenience. **Nobody competes on African luxury heritage narrative.** This is Studex's uncaptured moat.

### Opportunities for Studex
- Anchor the brand on the **Ankole story** in all marketing — competitors can't replicate this
- **Corporate gifting** is underserved — gift boxes at R1,200+ are a high-margin, low-competition niche
- Explore **Takealot** for distribution reach, but protect brand premium via separate SKUs

## TOPIC 2: SA B2B Coffee — Hotels, Restaurants, Offices

### Key Findings

- **B2B channel dominates coffee market** (59.8% of all roasted coffee sales globally). Cafés, hotels, offices, institutions are the anchor buyers.
- **Global B2B coffee growing at 4.8% CAGR** through 2033. Steady, not flashy.
- **SA B2B buyer profile:**
  - **Hotels/Restaurants (Horeca):** Willing to pay premiums for documented quality, traceability, and brand-aligned sourcing. Five-star properties already paying premiums for sustainable credentials.
  - **Corporate offices:** The "institutional coffee machine era" is ending. Forward-thinking companies (tech, finance, professional services) want sustainability-linked sourcing they can talk about.
  - **Key pain points:** Inconsistent quality, long lead times, lack of traceability, USD price volatility.
- **Snob's Coffee model (competitor reference):** B2B pitch has 3 layers — (1) quality/reliability, (2) narrative for menus and staff training, (3) ongoing partnership (tastings, training, equipment support). This is the right model for Studex Coffee.
- **Volume advantage:** Single corporate client with 500 employees = predictable recurring order. Smoother than retail.

### Opportunities for Studex
- **Farm-to-Horeca positioning** — full traceability, SA/Rwandan origin story, PROWTC certification. This is what hotel procurement officers want to be able to say.
- **Trial-to-contract model:** Cupping session → short trial supply → standing order. Standard B2B cycle.
- **Target第一批:** Boutique hotels in Sandton, Cape Town fin-tech corridor, export-facing game lodges.
- **CIIE November 2026 Shanghai** — Rwanda coffee buyers will be there. Apply for booth NOW.

## TOPIC 3: Voicebox — Open-Source AI Voice Studio

### What It Is
- **jamiepine/voicebox** — 39.9K GitHub stars, MIT license, local-first voice studio
- Replaces ElevenLabs (output) + WisprFlow (input) in one app
- **Runs locally** — no cloud, privacy-first, all data stays on the VM

### Key Capabilities
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Voice cloning | Zero-shot from a few seconds of audio. 50+ preset voices via Kokoro/Qwen |
| Languages | 23 languages — English, Arabic, Hindi, Swahili, Chinese, Japanese and more |
| TTS engines | Qwen3-TTS (best), Chatterbox Multilingual, LuxTTS, HumeAI TADA, Kokoro |
| Dictation | Global hotkey → speak anywhere → transcript pastes into any text field |
| Agent voice | MCP tool call (`voicebox.speak`) → any MCP-aware agent speaks in cloned voice |
| Post-processing | Pitch shift, reverb, chorus, compression — built in |
| API | REST + MCP server — install in Claude Code, Cursor, or any agent |

### Could Voicebox Power Studex Customer Service?

**YES — here's the architecture:**
```
Customer calls/WhatsApps → 
  Agent (Claude/OpenClaw) processes message → 
  Voicebox speaks response in Naledi's cloned voice → 
  Audio sent back via WhatsApp/Meta
```
- Clone Naledi's voice from a 10-second WhatsApp recording
- Voicebox runs on Orgo VM (Linux, CUDA-ready)
- Connect via REST `POST /speak` to OpenClaw session
- Instantiation: "Naledi speaks to customers" — CMO voice for outbound calls and responses

**Alternative (simpler):** Voicebox for inbound call IVR — "Press 1 to place an order, Press 2 for support" → routes to agent with voice confirmation.

### Risks
- Requires GPU on VM (8GB RAM Orgo VM — likely CPU only, may be slow for real-time)
- Not yet a production-grade call center replacement — experimental but credible
- Linux build requires source compilation (no pre-built binary yet)
