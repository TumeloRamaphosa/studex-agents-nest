## TOPIC 2: South Africa B2B Coffee Market

### Key Market Realities
- **Hotels & Restaurants (Horeca channel):** Primary buyers of premium coffee in SA. Johannesburg, Cape Town, Sandton, and Durban hotel groups are the core accounts. Buyers source via coffee distributors/wholesalers — rarely direct from farms.
- **Top distributors SA:** Tchibo, Illy, Starbucks at SEA, and local players like Deluxe Coffee, Coffee Tech, and Cape Town Coffee Roasters. Importers handle the supply chain.
- **Pain points buyers face:**
  - Inconsistent single-origin quality (big problem for specialty coffee)
  - Long lead times from international suppliers
  - Lack of traceability — want to know farm of origin
  - Price volatility on USD-denominated imports
- **Opportunity for Studex:** Robusca can position as the **farm-to-HORECA supplier** — direct relationships, full traceability, SA-grown specialty coffee. Target: boutique hotels in Sandton, restaurants doing "locally sourced" menus, export-facing lodges.

### UAE Coffee Opportunity (PROWTC Dubai link)
- UAE hospitality is a massive premium coffee buyer — Starbucks Reserve, specialty roasters, hotel groups
- Rwanda coffee is well-regioned in Middle East markets — wet-processed A1/A2 grades fit well
- PROWTC Dubai deal in progress: Rwanda Wet Polished A1 + A2 — this is the right product for this market
- **Key benchmark:** Rwandan coffee currently fetches $3–6/kg FOB in bulk. With premium grading (A1) + Gulf logistics, landed cost ~$5–8/kg. Mark to $12–18/kg for Horeca resale.
