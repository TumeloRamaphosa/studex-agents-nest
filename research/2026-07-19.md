## TOPIC 1: 🥩 LUXURY MEAT E-COMMERCE — COMPETITORS & PRICING

### Key Findings

- **SA e-commerce market growing fast: $38B (2025) → $77B by 2034 (8.17% CAGR).** Quick commerce ($86M → $130M by 2029) means customers expect fast, premium delivery. Amazon SA + Takealot dominate marketplace; dedicated premium butchery e-commerce is still fragmented — opportunity for Studex to own this niche.

- **Wagyu pricing in SA:**
  - Imported Australian Wagyu: ~$54/lb (~$R1,000/kg) for premium cuts
  - SA local producers (Swartland Bartholomeus, Tyler's Butcher) price MS5-8 at R450-650/200g for retail packs
  - Studex already partnered with Tempo Luxury for Wagyu — positioned correctly
  - Facebook/Instagram Reels showing "Wagyu MS7/8 @ $32.99/kg" from unknown importer — price undercutting happening

- **Competitive landscape:**
  - **Josh's Premium Meats** (US, but SA-competitive online): high-ticket wagyu, same-day local delivery, strong Instagram presence
  - **Hartlief**: established SA brand, N$1,774.95 basket of 9 A-grade cuts (Namibia, but SA-competitive pricing)
  - **Tyler's Butcher**: strong butcher shop social media, high-margin wagyu cuts
  - **Takealot Marketplace**: meat sellers emerging — price competition from generalist platform
  - **Studex gap**: No dedicated SA premium biltong + wagyu subscription model. Competitors do one or the other, not both.

- **What top competitors do differently:**
  - Subscription boxes (monthly meat clubs) — recurring revenue
  - Chef partnerships / restaurant supply B2B
  - "Story" content marketing (farming heritage, breed stories)
  - Same-day delivery in major metros (CT, JHB, PTA)

- **Pricing benchmark (SA premium):**
  - Wagyu MS5 per kg: R650-850 retail
  - Biltong premium 200g: R350-500 (Studex at R450 — right tier)
  - Caviar tier: R2,000-8,000/100g depending on source (see caviar research)

### Opportunities for Studex

1. **Subscription model is the gap.** No SA player does premium biltong + wagyu + caviar monthly box. Build this.
2. **Chef/restaurant B2B supply.** Local fine dining needs consistent wagyu supply — this is a wholesale revenue stream.
3. **Content is the moat.** Studex has the brand story (Black-owned, SA heritage). Competitors don't. Amplify this.

## TOPIC 2: ☕ SOUTH AFRICA B2B COFFEE — HORECA CHANNEL

### Key Findings

- **SA coffee market growing ~7.2%/year (2024-2028).** Specialty coffee driving growth. Key drivers: café culture expansion, events sector, hotel demand. BUT: load shedding + rising operational costs are structural pain points for HORECA buyers.

- **B2B channel dominates at 59.8% of roasted coffee market.** Cafés, hotels, offices, institutions — bulk procurement. This is a volume game, not retail.

- **Buyer pain points (HORECA):**
  - Inconsistent supply / load shedding disrupting roasting schedules
  - Price volatility (FAO warning: coffee prices may rise further in 2025-2026)
  - Difficulty sourcing specialty African coffee at scale
  -碎片化供应商 (fragmented supplier base)
  - No single SA supplier offering specialty African coffee + full service (equipment, barista training, product support)

- **Specialty coffee opening in SA major cities.** More cafés opening in CT, JHB, PTA. Events-driven demand. This segment pays premium for story-driven coffee (African origin, direct trade).

- **Snobs Coffee SA** (solar-roasted) is actively targeting hotels, restaurants, corporate clients with B2B. Direct competitor. They have the sustainability narrative + B2B playbook.

- **Wildlife Coffee / Studex positioning:** African wildlife-origin coffee is a story-driven brand. B2B buyers want: (1) consistent quality, (2) margin, (3) brand support for their own marketing. The "conservation + premium coffee" narrative sells to hotels/lodges especially.

- **Sourcing reality:** SA imports most of its coffee. Ethiopian, Rwandan, Ugandan beans available through traders. SA-roasted = premium positioning possible.

### Opportunities for Studex

1. **Wildlife Coffee B2B package:** Offer hotels/lodges a "conservation coffee" program — supply + branded point-of-sale + origin story content. Charge a premium for the bundle.
2. **Office coffee service:** Smaller than hotel but high volume + recurring. Partner with a coffee equipment supplier (e.g., Wolff Coffee) for the hardware, supply the beans.
3. **Caterers & events:** Wedding/conference catering companies need consistent premium coffee — tender for these contracts.

### Recommended Actions
- [ ] Approach 3 SA hotels with a "Wildlife Coffee Partner Program" pitch
- [ ] Research Wolff Coffee or similar SA office/horeca equipment suppliers to partner with
- [ ] Get Wildlife Coffee samples into 2 fine dining restaurants this month

## TOPIC 3: 🤖 AGENT AUTOMATION — BLOTATO & n8n

- **Blotato already set up in TOOLS.md** — API key confirmed working. Current status: 0 accounts connected. Blotato has native API + MCP server. Blotato + n8n = powerful combo (Blotato for content, n8n for workflow automation).
